# Time-Based Delay Patterns

## Monthly Patterns

### Best Months to Fly (Lowest Delays)
1. **September**: 14% delay rate
2. **October**: 15% delay rate
3. **November**: 16% delay rate

### Worst Months to Fly (Highest Delays)
1. **June**: 24% delay rate
2. **July**: 23% delay rate
3. **December**: 22% delay rate

## Weekly Patterns

### Best Days to Fly
1. **Saturday**: 15% delay rate
2. **Tuesday**: 17% delay rate
3. **Wednesday**: 18% delay rate

### Worst Days to Fly
1. **Thursday**: 22% delay rate
2. **Friday**: 21% delay rate
3. **Sunday**: 20% delay rate

## Hourly Patterns

### Best Times to Fly
- **5:00 - 7:00 AM**: 12% delay rate
- **7:00 - 9:00 AM**: 14% delay rate
- **9:00 - 11:00 AM**: 16% delay rate

### Worst Times to Fly
- **6:00 - 9:00 PM**: 28% delay rate
- **4:00 - 6:00 PM**: 25% delay rate
- **3:00 - 4:00 PM**: 22% delay rate

## Why Early Flights Are Better

1. **No cascading delays** - First flight of the day starts fresh
2. **Less air traffic** - Fewer planes competing for runways
3. **Better weather visibility** - Morning fog clears by departure
4. **Crew is rested** - Pilots and crew starting their shift

## Holiday Travel Patterns

### Highest Delay Periods
- Thanksgiving week: 26% delay rate
- Christmas week: 25% delay rate
- Summer peak (July 4th): 24% delay rate

### Tips for Holiday Travel
- Book early morning flights
- Fly on the actual holiday (fewer travelers)
- Avoid connecting flights if possible
